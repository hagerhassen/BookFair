Authors & Self Publishing

Authors feel at home at the Fair – and so they should. Author HQ, comprising a theatre and networking area, is one of the most popular features of The Book Fair, attracting a great deal of interest from the self-publishing community.
Additionally, Author HQ demonstrates BF’s role in acknowledging the changes in the industry, by providing a forum for discussion of the ever expanding number of approaches to publishing, including many exciting initiatives such as agent meetings and panel style "pitch slam" events.
Our Author of the Day programme, meanwhile, helps bring writers face-to-face with reputable and bestselling authors who will share their journey to publication during a special series of talks at the Fair.
Outside of the Fair, Authors can find inspiration and network with their peers at our day-long conference, The Writer's Summit, held in November.

Authors

There's many authors and writers take part in Bookfairs every year from all over world.
Arabic authors like Anis Mansour,Hussien Abdlqodos,Yehia Hakky,Ahmed Khaled Tawfik, Aser Abdallah Elnashimy,Ahlam Mosteghanemi,Yousef Elsebaay and Yousef Edris from Cairo Book fair , Alexandria Book fair and Dubai Book fair.
Foreigner authors like Agatha Christie and William Shakespeare.
Author HQ is always one of the most popular features at the Fair. 2018 saw it in its sixth year, both bigger and better than ever with seminars and events throughout the Fair as well as the return of The Write Stuff, which offered aspiring writers the chance to pitch their book to a panel of agents.
And there is The Author Club that gives its members access to author related news and activities across the entire year – including a free visitor ticket to The London Book Fair. Additional benefits include a monthly newsletter which includes discounts and early bird offers to attend author events
